## Instructions for This Section

**Purpose:** Share noteworthy data, research, and trends that support the week's themes. Make readers curious about what's changing in education.

**Structure for Each Item:**
1. **Headline** (hyperlinked): Compelling title that hints at the insight
2. **Description** (1-2 sentences): Context, data, or relevant stat
3. **Source attribution** (parentheses): Publisher/author/organization name

**Requirements:**
- Must be data-driven (research, statistics, measurable trends, policy changes)
- NOT just stories or philosophy (save those for Parting Thought)
- Each item must have a newsworthy angle
- Include specific numbers when available (followers, views, percentage)
- Reference source in parentheses at end
- Hyperlink the title to the original article/resource

**Tone:**
- Pirate Wires style: observational, slightly irreverent
- Lean into the contrast or irony (teachers dismissed ambitions → now she's winning pitch competitions)
- Make the data human (1.6M followers means people are hungry for authentic teaching)
- Tease without explaining (don't summarize the whole article)

**Length:** 1-2 sentences per item + source attribution

**Source Attribution Format:**
- `(Source Name)` for single source: `(Rick Hess, AEI)`
- `(Publication Name)` for aggregated: `(The 74 Million)`
- `(Name, Organization)` for research: `(Liz Cohen's research, The 74 Million)`

**Key Characteristics:**
- Titles are punchy and specific ("Builds 2.4M Followers" not just "Viral Teacher")
- Each includes a surprising stat or angle (1.6M followers, $1B goal, 1M views)
- Written to make readers want to click and learn more
- Mix of education topics: teacher culture, student entrepreneurship, design, curriculum, research, policy
- Source attribution includes credible publications (AEI, Austin Scholar, The 74 Million, Libertas Institute)
